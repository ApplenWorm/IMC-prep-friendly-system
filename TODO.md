IMC PREPARATION TODO CHECKLIST
==============================

Use this file to **track your progress** through the phases described in `PLAN.md`.  
Tick items with `[x]` as you complete them.

You do **not** need to do everything linearly; you can be in different phases for different subjects. Treat this as a menu of concrete milestones.

---

## Phase 0 — Mathematical Maturity & Proof Fluency (Weeks 1–2)

- **Foundations of proof**
  - [ ] Read **“How to Solve It” by George Pólya** (at least Part I carefully).
  - [ ] Write at least **10** proofs using methods from Pólya (induction, contradiction, invariants).
  - [ ] Solve **10+** elementary proof problems from your lecture notes or online sheets.

- **First rigorous analysis exposure**
  - [ ] Read introductory chapters of **“Understanding Analysis (2nd ed.)” by Stephen Abbott** or **“Principles of Mathematical Analysis (3rd ed.)” by Walter Rudin**.
  - [ ] Prove the **Bolzano–Weierstrass theorem** on your own.
  - [ ] Prove **Cauchy–Schwarz inequality** from first principles.

- **Diagnostic**
  - [ ] Attempt 3–5 problems from a recent **Putnam** (any year) — record how many you solve fully.

## Phase 1 — Linear Algebra & Real Analysis Foundations (Weeks 3–8)

### Linear Algebra Track

- **Core theory: “Linear Algebra Done Right (3rd ed.)” — Sheldon Axler**
  - [ ] Finish Chapters on vector spaces, linear maps, and eigenvalues.
  - [ ] Finish the chapter on **inner product spaces** and **orthogonality**.
  - [ ] Finish the chapter covering **spectral theorem**.
  - [ ] Complete at least **70%** of the exercises from these chapters.

- **Supplement: “Linear Algebra (2nd ed.)” — Kenneth Hoffman & Ray Kunze**
  - [ ] Read sections on determinants and characteristic polynomials.
  - [ ] Study rational and **Jordan canonical form** (even if only conceptually).
  - [ ] Solve **20+** problems involving diagonalization, minimal polynomials, and canonical forms.

- **Problem book: “Problems and Theorems in Linear Algebra” — V.V. Prasolov**
  - [ ] Solve **25** problems from the basic sections (vector spaces, linear maps).
  - [ ] Solve **25** problems involving eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and minimal polynomials.
  - [ ] Solve **20** problems involving canonical forms or spectral theorem applications.

### Real Analysis Track

- **Core theory: “Understanding Analysis (2nd ed.)” — Stephen Abbott**
  - [ ] Finish chapters on sequences and series of real numbers.
  - [ ] Finish chapters on continuity and differentiation.
  - [ ] Finish chapters on Riemann integration and sequences of functions.
  - [ ] Do at least **60%** of end-of-chapter exercises.

- **Reference: “Principles of Mathematical Analysis (3rd ed.)” — Walter Rudin**
  - [ ] Read metric-space sections (compactness, completeness, connectedness).
  - [ ] Attempt **15+** Rudin exercises on sequences, series, and continuity.

- **Problem book: “Problems in Real Analysis: Advanced Calculus on the Real Axis” — Radulescu, Radulescu & Andreescu**
  - [ ] Solve **15** problems on sequences and limits.
  - [ ] Solve **15** problems on continuity and differentiability.
  - [ ] Solve **15** problems on integrals and series.

- **Phase 1 diagnostic**
  - [ ] Attempt **5** analysis and **5** linear algebra problems from **“Berkeley Problems in Mathematics (3rd ed.)”**.
  - [ ] For each unsolved problem, read and summarize the official solution.

## Phase 2 — Abstract Algebra, Complex Analysis, Combinatorics (Weeks 9–13)

### Abstract Algebra

- **Core theory**
  - [ ] Read group-theory chapters (1–6) in **“Abstract Algebra (3rd ed.)” by Dummit & Foote**.
  - [ ] Solve **20** group-theory exercises from Dummit & Foote or **“Topics in Algebra (2nd ed.)” by I.N. Herstein**.
  - [ ] Study rings and ideals in **Dummit & Foote** (Chapters 7–9).
  - [ ] Solve **15** exercises on polynomial rings and ideals.

- **Key concepts checklist**
  - [ ] Comfortable with homomorphisms and isomorphism theorems for groups.
  - [ ] Comfortable with quotient groups and quotient rings.
  - [ ] Can state and use the **Sylow theorems**.

### Complex Analysis

- **Core theory**
  - [ ] Read main chapters of **“Complex Analysis” by Lars Ahlfors** or **“Complex Analysis (3rd ed.)” by Bak & Newman**.
  - [ ] Work through examples of contour integrals and residue computations.
  - [ ] Understand isolated singularities (removable, poles, essential) and Laurent series.

- **Problem practice**
  - [ ] Solve **20** problems from **“Problems and Solutions for Complex Analysis” by Rami Shakarchi**.
  - [ ] Solve **20** problems from **“A Collection of Problems on Complex Analysis” by L.I. Volkovyskii et al.**

### Combinatorics

- **Core theory**
  - [ ] Read combinatorics basics in **“A Walk Through Combinatorics (4th ed.)” by Miklós Bóna** (counting, inclusion–exclusion).
  - [ ] Read generating functions chapters in **“Generatingfunctionology (3rd ed.)” by Herbert Wilf**.
  - [ ] Read graph-theory basics in **Bóna** or **“Combinatorics: Topics, Techniques, Algorithms” by Peter Cameron**.

- **Problems**
  - [ ] Solve **25** counting and inclusion–exclusion problems from **A Walk Through Combinatorics**.
  - [ ] Solve **20** generating-function problems from **Generatingfunctionology**.
  - [ ] Solve **15** graph-theory or extremal problems from **Ioan Tomescu’s Problems in Combinatorics and Graph Theory** or similar.

## Ongoing Habits (All Phases)

- **Techniques notebook**
  - [ ] Create a dedicated **techniques notebook** (paper or digital).
  - [ ] For every new trick you encounter, add a one-paragraph explanation plus a model problem.
  - [ ] Review the notebook at least **once every 2 weeks**.

- **Writing and exposition**
  - [ ] For at least **20** problems (across phases), write full LaTeX solutions as if submitting to a grader.
  - [ ] Ask a friend/mentor (or your future self after a week) to critique clarity and rigor.

- **Community and external resources**
  - [ ] Use the **Art of Problem Solving (AoPS) forum** to read or discuss at least **10** IMC or Putnam problem threads.
  - [ ] Browse **American Mathematical Monthly** / **Mathematics Magazine** / **College Mathematics Journal** problem sections and attempt **10** problems.
