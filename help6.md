Now I'll create a comprehensive, well-structured preparation guide for the IMC.Here's your complete IMC preparation guide. Here's a summary of what's inside across all 7 parts:

---

**Part I — The Competition** covers exactly what IMC is: two 5-hour exams, 5 proof-based problems each, from Algebra, Real/Complex Analysis, Combinatorics, and Geometry. It also contextualizes the difficulty (harder than Putnam, easier than Schweitzer) and how scoring/medals work.

**Part II — Foundations Before Problems** breaks down *what theory you need to know cold* before touching any problem book — by topic: linear algebra (spectral theorem, Jordan form, Cayley-Hamilton), real analysis (metric spaces, uniform convergence, Riemann integration), abstract algebra (group actions, Sylow, ring/field theory), complex analysis (residues, Cauchy, maximum modulus), and combinatorics (generating functions, probabilistic method).

**Part III — Theory Textbooks** gives you the best learning texts per topic before competition drilling:
- **Linear Algebra**: Axler (*Done Right*), Hoffman-Kunze, Prasolov
- **Real Analysis**: Abbott, Rudin, Apostol, Radulescu-Andreescu
- **Abstract Algebra**: Dummit-Foote, Herstein, Artin
- **Complex Analysis**: Ahlfors, Bak-Newman, Andreescu
- **Combinatorics**: Bóna, Wilf (*Generatingfunctionology*), Alon-Spencer

**Part IV — Competition Problem Books** covers the full competition-caliber library beyond your Berkeley book: *Putnam and Beyond* (Gelca-Andreescu, your most important purchase), *Problems from the Book* (Andreescu-Dospinescu), *Contests in Higher Mathematics* (Miklós Schweitzer), Bollobás's *The Art of Mathematics*, Pólya-Szegő's *Problems and Theorems in Analysis*, all four Putnam volumes, Prasolov's polynomials book, and more.

**Part V — Online Resources** covers Cezar Lupu's free worksheets, MIT OCW, past IMC papers, the Vojtech Jarník competition, AoPS, and Bristol's actual IMC training materials.

**Part VI — The 52-Week Plan** maps out 5 phases (proof maturity → linear algebra/analysis theory → other theory → core problem books → full IMC simulation) with a concrete weekly structure and a diagnostic to tell you where to jump in.
