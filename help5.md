# Complete IMC Preparation Guide & Resource Library

## Understanding the IMC Competition

The **International Mathematics Competition (IMC)** for university students is held annually in Blagoevgrad, Bulgaria. According to the official website :

- **Participants**: Undergraduate students completing their 1st, 2nd, 3rd, or 4th year
- **Age limit**: Maximum 23 years (with some exceptions possible)
- **Format**: Two days, 10 problems total (5 each day)
- **Topics**: Algebra, Real and Complex Analysis, Geometry, and Combinatorics
- **Language**: English

The competition problems are at the undergraduate level but require creative problem-solving rather than just routine calculations .

## Core Problem Books (Similar to Berkeley Problems)

Since you already have **"Berkeley Problems in Mathematics"** by Paulo Ney de Souza and Jorge-Nuno Silva, here are additional comprehensive problem books in the same format:

### 1. **Putnam and Beyond** (2nd Edition) — Titu Andreescu & Răzvan Gelca 
- **Why it's essential**: Specifically designed for undergraduate competitions
- **Coverage**: Algebra, Real Analysis, Geometry/Trigonometry, Number Theory, Combinatorics, Probability
- **Problems**: Over 1,300 problems with complete solutions
- **Structure**: Each chapter presents theory followed by clustered problems by topic
- **Best for**: Systematic preparation across all IMC topics

### 2. **Problem Solving Through Problems** — Loren C. Larson 
- **Level**: Advanced undergraduate
- **Focus**: Problem-solving strategies and methodology
- **Topics**: Covers algebra, analysis, combinatorics, and geometry
- **Style**: Emphasizes techniques over specific content knowledge

### 3. **Problems and Solutions in Mathematics** — Ta-Tsien Li et al. 
- **Format**: Similar to Berkeley Problems — generalist approach
- **Coverage**: Various branches including algebra, analysis, topology, differential equations
- **Source**: Problems from prestigious university exams

### 4. **A Collection of Math Competition Problems** — Slobodan Filipovski (2024) 
- **Content**: IMC and Vojtech Jarnik Competition problems (2013–2023)
- **Availability**: Open access PDF from University of Primorska
- **Bonus**: Includes Team Selection Test problems and original authored problems

## Structured 6-Month Preparation Plan

### **Phase 1: Foundation Building (Weeks 1–8)**

**Goal**: Master core undergraduate material and basic problem-solving techniques.

**Week 1–2: Proof Techniques & Methodology**
- Study: "How to Solve It" by Pólya (Chapters 1–4)
- Study: "The Art and Craft of Problem Solving" by Zeitz (Part I: The Problems)
- Practice: Basic proof techniques from Durham University sheets 
- Daily: 2–3 problems focusing on induction, contradiction, pigeonhole principle

**Week 3–4: Algebra Fundamentals**
- Study: "101 Problems in Algebra" (first 50 problems)
- Review: Linear algebra (vector spaces, eigenvalues, canonical forms)
- Study: Polynomial theory and equations
- Daily: 3–4 algebra problems

**Week 5–6: Real Analysis Basics**
- Study: "Counterexamples in Analysis" (select chapters)
- Review: Limits, continuity, differentiation, integration
- Study: Sequences and series convergence
- Daily: 3–4 analysis problems

**Week 7–8: Geometry & Combinatorics Introduction**
- Study: "Challenging Problems in Geometry" (first half)
- Study: "A Path to Combinatorics for Undergraduates" (Chapters 1–3)
- Review: Euclidean geometry, counting principles
- Daily: Mixed problems from both areas

### **Phase 2: Intensive Problem Solving (Weeks 9–16)**

**Goal**: Work through comprehensive problem books and build stamina.

**Week 9–10: Putnam and Beyond — Algebra & Analysis**
- Complete: Chapters on Algebra (matrices, polynomials) and Real Analysis
- Focus: Understanding solution techniques, not just answers
- Daily: 5–6 problems with full write-ups

**Week 11–12: Putnam and Beyond — Geometry & Number Theory**
- Complete: Geometry/Trigonometry and Number Theory chapters
- Supplement: "104 Number Theory Problems" for number theory depth
- Daily: 5–6 problems with full write-ups

**Week 13–14: Putnam and Beyond — Combinatorics & Probability**
- Complete: Combinatorics and Probability chapters
- Supplement: "102 Combinatorial Problems"
- Daily: 5–6 problems with full write-ups

**Week 15–16: Berkeley Problems in Mathematics**
- Work through: Problems by subject area
- Focus: Problems from 2000–2003 (most recent in your edition)
- Note: 4th edition coming out with updated problems 
- Daily: 6–8 problems, timed when possible

### **Phase 3: Competition Simulation (Weeks 17–22)**

**Goal**: Practice under competition conditions and identify weak areas.

**Week 17–18: Past IMC Problems (Recent Years)**
- Source: imc-math.org.uk problem archive 
- Practice: 2022–2024 problems
- Format: Simulate 2-day competition (5 problems/day, 4 hours/day)
- Review: Solutions carefully, noting alternative approaches

**Week 19–20: Vojtech Jarnik Competition Problems**
- Source: vjimc.osu.cz
- Practice: Problems from 2015–2024
- Focus: This competition has similar difficulty and topics to IMC

**Week 21: Weak Area Intensive**
- Identify: Your weakest topics from previous weeks
- Study: Relevant chapters from topic-specific books
- Practice: 10+ problems in weak areas only

**Week 22: Mixed Practice**
- Source: "A Collection of Math Competition Problems" by Filipovski 
- Practice: Team Selection Test problems
- Format: Timed sessions with full solutions

### **Phase 4: Final Preparation (Weeks 23–26)**

**Week 23–24: Full Mock Competitions**
- Practice: Complete IMC papers from 2018–2024
- Conditions: Strict timing, no interruptions, write complete solutions
- Review: Have solutions checked by mentor or compare with official solutions

**Week 25: Review & Refinement**
- Review: All mistakes from previous 6 months
- Study: Elegant solutions from "Putnam and Beyond"
- Practice: Quick problems to maintain speed

**Week 26: Light Preparation**
- Practice: 1–2 problems daily to stay sharp
- Focus: Rest and mental preparation
- Review: Key theorems and formulas

## Key Strategies for Success

1. **Solution Quality Over Quantity**: Write complete, rigorous solutions. IMC requires proof-based answers, not just answers .

2. **Study Solutions Carefully**: Even when you solve a problem, read the book's solution — it often shows more elegant approaches .

3. **Build Intuition**: Before attacking a problem, understand what it's asking. Pólya's "How to Solve It" framework helps .

4. **Time Management**: In competition, if stuck after 15–20 minutes, move on and return later.

5. **Collaborate**: Join or form a problem-solving group. Durham University's approach emphasizes collective effort .

6. **Cross-Reference**: Use multiple books for the same topic — different perspectives deepen understanding.
