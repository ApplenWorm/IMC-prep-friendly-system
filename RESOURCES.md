## 1. Core Theory Textbooks (Study Before Problem Books)

### 1.1 Linear Algebra

- **Linear Algebra Done Right (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Sheldon Axler*  
  The gold standard for conceptual linear algebra. Avoids determinants initially and builds theory through linear maps and eigenvalues.  

- **Linear Algebra (2nd ed.)** — *Author: Kenneth Hoffman & Ray Kunze*  
  Classical, rigorous treatment of linear algebra. Strong on determinants, canonical forms, and inner product spaces.  

- **Problems and Theorems in Linear Algebra** — *Author: V.V. Prasolov*  
  Dense problem-theorem style book; bridges theory and competition.  

- **Matrix Analysis** — *Author: Roger Horn & Charles Johnson*  
  Graduate-level; for deep spectral theory, inequalities, and advanced matrix methods.  

- **Advanced Linear Algebra** — *Author: Steven Roman*  
  Abstract linear algebra; helpful background for the hardest IMC algebra problems.  

- **Linear Algebra Problem Book** — *Author: Paul R. Halmos*  
  Linear algebra developed entirely via problems and hints.  

---

### 1.2 Real Analysis

- **Understanding Analysis (2nd ed.)** — *Author: Stephen Abbott*  
  Very readable rigorous introduction; recommended by Bristol IMC training.  

- **Principles of Mathematical Analysis (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Walter Rudin*  
  “Baby Rudin” — classic, terse but comprehensive reference.  

- **Mathematical Analysis (2nd ed.)** — *Author: Tom Apostol*  
  Comprehensive, including multivariable analysis and an introduction to Lebesgue theory.  

- **Real Analysis** — *Author: Royden & Fitzpatrick*  
  Standard text including measure theory and Lebesgue integration.  

- **Problems in Real Analysis: Advanced Calculus on the Real Axis** — *Author: Radulescu, Radulescu & Andreescu*  
  Competition-style problem book organized by topic.  

- **Problems in Mathematical Analysis (Volumes 1, 2, and 3)** — *Author: W.J. Kaczor and M.T. Nowak*  
  Massive problem collections on sequences, continuity/differentiation, and integration.  

- **A Problem Book in Real Analysis** — *Author: Asuman G. Aksoy and Mohamed A. Khamsi*  
  Additional real-analysis problems and solutions.  

- **Counterexamples in Analysis** — *Author: Gelbaum & Olmsted*  
  Builds intuition through pathological examples.  

---

### 1.3 Abstract Algebra

- **Abstract Algebra (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Dummit & Foote*  
  Comprehensive standard reference on groups, rings, fields, and modules.  

- **Topics in Algebra (2nd ed.)** — *Author: I.N. Herstein*  
  Elegant, exercise-heavy book, especially strong on groups.  

- **Algebra** — *Author: Michael Artin*  
  Geometric-algebraic approach; excellent on group actions and symmetry.  

- **Problems in Higher Algebra** — *Author: D.K. Faddeev and I.S. Sominsky*  
  Classic problem book with many competition-level algebra problems.  

- **Algebra: A Chapter of the Book** — *Author: Titu Andreescu and Gabriel Dospinescu*  
  Competition-flavored algebra problems with commentary.  

---

### 1.4 Complex Analysis

- **Complex Analysis** — *Author: Elias M. Stein and Rami Shakarchi*  
  Problem-focused graduate-level complex analysis.  

- **Complex Analysis** — *Author: Lars Ahlfors*  
  Classic rigorous complex analysis text.  

- **Complex Analysis (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Joseph Bak & Donald Newman*  
  Accessible, computation-oriented complex analysis.  

- **Visual Complex Analysis** — *Author: Tristan Needham*  
  Geometry-heavy, intuition-building complex analysis.  

- **Complex Numbers from A to Z** — *Author: Titu Andreescu & Dorin Andrica*  
  Competition-focused complex-number techniques, often applied to geometry.  

- **Problems and Solutions for Complex Analysis** — *Author: Rami Shakarchi*  
  Problem book complementing complex analysis theory.  

- **A Collection of Problems on Complex Analysis** — *Author: L.I. Volkovyskii, et al.*  
  Classical large set of complex-analysis exercises.  

---

### 1.5 Combinatorics & Discrete Mathematics

- **A Walk Through Combinatorics (4th ed.)** — *Author: Miklós Bóna*  
  Introductory yet rigorous, with many problems.  

- **Combinatorics: Topics, Techniques, Algorithms** — *Author: Peter Cameron*  
  More advanced; includes design theory, codes, finite geometry.  

- **A Path to Combinatorics for Undergraduates: Counting Strategies** — *Author: Titu Andreescu and Zuming Feng*  
  Bridges basic counting to olympiad-level combinatorics.  

- **Generatingfunctionology (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Herbert Wilf*  
  Definitive text on generating functions (available free online).  

- **The Probabilistic Method (4th ed.)** — *Author: Alon & Spencer*  
  Introduces probabilistic arguments in combinatorics and graph theory.  

- **102 Combinatorial Problems** — *Author: Titu Andreescu and Zuming Feng*  
  Competition-level combinatorics problems.  

- **102 Combinatorial Problems from the Training of the USA IMO Team** — *Author: Titu Andreescu & Zuming Feng*  
  Focused combinatorics training problems with solutions.  

- **Problem-Solving Methods in Combinatorics: An Approach to Olympiad Problems** — *Author: Pablo Soberon*  
  Olympiad-focused combinatorics methods.  

- **Graph Theory: In Mathematical Olympiad and Competitions** — *Author: Xiong Bin, Zheng Zhongyi*  
  Competition-style graph theory.  

- **Problems in Combinatorics and Graph Theory** — *Author: Ioan Tomescu*  
  Classical collection used in Romanian training.  

- **Graph Theory** — *Author: Reinhard Diestel*  
  Standard graduate text (freely available online).

## 2. General Problem-Solving & Competition Problem Books

### 2.1 “Berkeley Problems”–Style and Multi-topic Problem Books

- **Berkeley Problems in Mathematics (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Paulo Ney de Souza & Jorge-Nuno Silva*  
  UC Berkeley PhD qualifying exam problems and solutions across analysis, algebra, and more.  

- **Putnam and Beyond (2nd ed.)** — *Author: Razvan Gelca & Titu Andreescu*  
  Central competition problem book for IMC/Putnam-level problems across all key topics.  

- **Problem-Solving Through Problems** — *Author: Loren C. Larson*  
  Focuses on problem-solving strategies with many Putnam-level problems.  

- **Problem-Solving Strategies** — *Author: Arthur Engel*  
  Encyclopedic collection of problems organized by technique.  

- **The Art and Craft of Problem Solving (3rd ed.)** — *Author: Paul Zeitz*  
  Heuristic and mindset guide with many problems.  

- **Problems from the Book (2nd ed.)** — *Author: Titu Andreescu & Gabriel Dospinescu*  
  Hard, beautiful problems with elegant solutions.  

- **The Art of Mathematics: Coffee Time in Memphis** — *Author: Béla Bollobás*  
  157 elegant problems from across mathematics.  

- **The Math Problems Notebook** — *Author: Valentin Boju, Louis Funar*  
  Advanced competition problems.  

- **Problems and Theorems in Analysis I and II** — *Author: George Pólya and Gábor Szegő*  
  Legendary analysis problem collections.  

- **Problems in Mathematical Analysis (Volumes I, II, III)** — *Author: W.J. Kaczor and M.T. Nowak*  
  (Also listed above; foundational problem series.)  

- **(Almost) Impossible Integrals, Sums, and Series** — *Author: Cornel Ioan Vălean*  
  Very challenging integrals and series, often requiring advanced techniques.

## 4. Online Resources, Courses & Lecture Notes

### 4.1 Past Competition Problems and Archives

- **IMC official site**: `imc-math.org.uk` — all past problems and solutions since 1994.  
- **Kiran Kedlaya's Putnam archive**: `kskedlaya.org/putnam-archive` — all Putnam problems with solutions, searchable.  
- **Vojtech Jarník Competition**: archives of problems and solutions at `vjimc.osu.cz` and related links in your notes.  
- **Miklós Schweitzer Memorial Competition**: problems and solutions, often via `vdoc.pub` and other PDF collections.  
- **USAMO / ISL shortlists**: upper-end problems with IMC-level difficulty in algebra/combinatorics.  
- **American Mathematical Monthly (AMM) Problem Section** — monthly competition-level problems; archives via JSTOR.  
- **Mathematics Magazine** and **College Mathematics Journal** — additional sources of contest-style problems.  
- **OMOUS (Open Mathematical Olympiad for University Students)** — Eastern European university competition, problems online.  
- **Romanian Mathematical Olympiad for University Students** — problem sets via Romanian mathematical journals.

## 6. Other Competitions to Use for Training

- **William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition** — American undergraduate competition, 6 hours, 12 problems, held annually in December.  
- **Vojtech Jarník International Mathematical Competition** — Czech/Slovak university competition.  
- **Miklós Schweitzer Memorial Competition** — Hungarian open-book competition, extremely hard.  
- **SEEMOUS (South Eastern European Mathematical Olympiad for University Students)** — regional competition at IMC-like level.  
- **Virginia Tech Regional Mathematics Contest (VTRMC)** — shorter contest, good for warm-up.  
- **USAMO / ISL Shortlist**, **American Mathematical Monthly Problems**, and other problem sections as ongoing practice.  

These, plus the books above, form your **complete IMC preparation resource library**.
